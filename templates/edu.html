All About Eclipses

Eclipses are captivating celestial events that occur when the Sun, Moon, and Earth align in precise arrangements, casting awe-inspiring shadows and producing breathtaking cosmic phenomena. These celestial occurrences have fascinated humanity for centuries, giving rise to myths, legends, and scientific discoveries.

There are two primary types of eclipses: solar eclipses and lunar eclipses. Solar eclipses transpire when the Moon temporarily obscures the Sun, plunging parts of the Earth into eerie darkness. On the other hand, lunar eclipses manifest when the Earth's shadow falls upon the Moon, transforming it into a captivating crimson hue.

Within these main categories lie various subtypes, each with its unique characteristics, such as total solar eclipses, partial lunar eclipses, and more. These cosmic phenomena have left a profound impact on cultures, societies, and scientific exploration throughout history.
