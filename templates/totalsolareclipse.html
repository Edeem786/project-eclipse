Total Solar Eclipse

A total solar eclipse is a breathtaking celestial event that occurs when the Moon completely covers the Sun, casting a shadow on the Earth. During this phenomenon, the Moon perfectly aligns with the Sun, blocking its light and creating a temporary twilight effect on the Earth.
What sets a total solar eclipse apart is the mesmerizing view of the Sun's outer atmosphere, known as the solar corona, which becomes visible as a halo of pearly-white wisps surrounding the obscured Sun. This rare event provides scientists with a unique opportunity to study the Sun's corona and its magnetic fields, as well as offering an inspiring spectacle for skywatchers.
