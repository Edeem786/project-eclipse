Total Lunar Eclipse


A total lunar eclipse is a mesmerizing celestial event that occurs when the Earth's shadow completely covers the Moon, giving it a deep reddish or copper hue.
This phenomenon occurs during a full moon when the Earth is positioned directly between the Sun and the Moon. The Earth's atmosphere scatters shorter wavelengths of light, allowing longer wavelengths (red and orange) to reach the Moon, creating the captivating lunar eclipse.
