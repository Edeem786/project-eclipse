Annular Solar Eclipse

An annular solar eclipse is a captivating celestial event in which the Moon partially covers the Sun, leaving a ring-like appearance of the Sun's outer edge, often referred to as the "Ring of Fire."
Unlike a total solar eclipse, in an annular eclipse, the Moon is farther from the Earth, and its apparent size is smaller, resulting in the Sun's outer edges being visible. This unique phenomenon offers an extraordinary visual experience for skywatchers.
