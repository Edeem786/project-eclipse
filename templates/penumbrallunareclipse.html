Penumbral Lunar Eclipse

A penumbral lunar eclipse is a subtle celestial event where the Moon passes through the Earth's penumbral shadow, resulting in a faint shading of the lunar surface.
Unlike partial or total lunar eclipses, a penumbral eclipse is more challenging to observe since the shading is subtle. Nevertheless, it is an intriguing astronomical event that occurs when the Earth, Moon, and Sun align in a specific way.
